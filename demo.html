Your official to-do list.
No stress.

We broke down everything you need to legally live and work in Chile — in the order you actually need to do it.

✅ Your Arrival Checklist

Get your RUT (tax ID)

Visit the SII office or do it at the Registro Civil. You'll need your passport + visa. This is your key to everything.

Open a bank account

BancoEstado is easiest — they accept RUT and passport. Required for rent payments and salary deposits.

Register at your local municipality

Go to your Municipalidad for local ID, voting registration, and access to local services.

Get FONASA (public health)

Register at any FONASA office with your RUT + contract. Gives you access to public hospitals and subsidized care.

AFP (pension registration)

If employed, your employer handles this. Choose between AFP Capital, Habitat, Provida, or Cuprum.

Carné de identidad (Chilean ID)

After permanent residency — visit Registro Civil for your Chilean ID. Takes 30 days to process.

Driver's license revalidation

Foreign licenses are valid for 90 days. After that, get a Chilean one at SERNAC with your docs.

📅 Visa Timeline

1
Day 1
Tourist Entry (90 days)

Enter Chile on a tourist visa. You have 90 days to find work or start your residency process.

2
Week 1–4
Apply for Temporary Residency

Submit online at extranjeria.gob.cl — you'll need passport, photos, apostilled birth certificate, and proof of accommodation.

3
Month 2–6
Wait for approval + RUT

Processing takes 2–6 months. In the meantime, get a provisional RUT from the SII to start working.

4
Year 1–2
Temporary Residency (1–2 yrs)

Live and work legally. Renew annually. Keep records of employment and no criminal record.

5
Year 2+
Permanent Residency

After 2 years of legal residence, apply for permanent residency — no more renewals, full rights.
